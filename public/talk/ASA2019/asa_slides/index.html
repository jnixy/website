Background

Official data on OIS are “a national embarassment” (1)

Rise of crowdsourced data - WAPO, The Guardian, Fatal Encounters, Mapping Police Violence… - Accompanying increase in studies utilizing these data (2-5) - …Accompanying increase in our understanding of OIS?

BUT NEVER FORGET: - All of these databases only track fatal OIS

Implications

We need to know about every time an officer shoots at someone - Ideally, every time they point their guns (8) - Many major cities are leading the way in terms of transparency

Rething waiting for EMS? - Difference between life and death can be minutes - EMTs often administer painkillers, which can make it difficult for surgeons to communicate with victims

Researchers: remember what we can and cannot conclude by analyzing crowdsourced fatal OIS data
